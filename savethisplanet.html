THE PROBLEM

What is going wrong with our broken oceans?

Beaches that used to be beautiful and covered with fine, white sand only decades ago are now littered with waste - most commonly, plastic. It is estimated that about 18 tons of plastic lie on our beaches today; this does not even include what can be found in our oceans. Flowing into our oceans, rivers bring with them close to 8 million tons of plastic material every year. Scientists and researchers have been warning us for years about the dangers of this level of plastic entering our oceans. It has a devastatingly negative effect on the marine life and health of our oceans overall. The biggest of the problems that come with having plastics fill our oceans is the problem that you cannot see with the naked eye. Microplastics are causing extreme alarms in the toxicology and marine biology worlds. These are pieces of plastic formed by the disintegration of plastic debris and are measured in fractions of a millimetre. Because they are so small, marine wildlife often consume them by mistake. Inside the bodies of these animals, they act as poisons. They are not able to digest these microplastics naturally and this leads to the death of many marine animals.

For the first time, the public is taking notice of these attrocities. The rise of the digital age has made these problems more visible. Social media is powerful and is shaping the way we look at the world. Heart breaking images captured by professional photographers are going viral. While it may seem petty, these images tell a story and sharing them with the world is only going to push us forward. Take a stance and be an advocate for change in your communities. Facebook, Tweet, and Insta with the hashtag #SaveThePlanet!
